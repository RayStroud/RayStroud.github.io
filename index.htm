About

Math, science, and technology have always been my passions. Naturally, I went into engineering after high school, which I loved. I found my calling, however, in an introductory programming class. Coding combines everything I love about math, science, and engineering. It's using technology to solve problems and improve lives.

That led me to the Northern Alberta Institute of Technology (NAIT) to the Digital Media and IT (DMIT) program , which has several sub-programs to learn about the IT and digital media industries. I concentrated on back-end web development and database, but I took extra classes to learn about front-end web development, game development, and some systems administration.

Concepts

Responsive

As someone who browses as much on a smartphone as a computer, I think websites need to be optimized for mobile browsing. Click here in a desktop browser to see how this page looks on a mobile device.

Templating

Separating layout, style, and content just makes sense. With Android, I've followed the built-in method of using XML. For the web, I've used ASP.NET's built in tools, as well as Handlerbars, an extension of Mustache.

Games

Playing games is great for relaxing, when there's a moment to spare. I've worked with XNA, Unity, and libGDX to build mini-games. The "create, update, draw" cycle is very familiar.

Teaching

I like to teach people about technology, either by helping with their smartphones, or by collaborating on a new programming idea. In the past, I've done mini-tutorials to teach a task or concept.

Leadership

Helping people to stay organized and on track comes naturally to me. In fact, for my final group project at NAIT, I took on the responsibility of project leader to organize the group's tasks.

Problem Solving

All that is needed to overcome most problems is a search engine, some time, and/or a little imagination. I thrive on finding simple and unique solutions to problems.

Philosophy

I believe in innovation, which doesn't just mean to use new technology. It also means to use old technology, reimagine it, and apply it to new situations, concepts, and ideas.

We live in a mobile world. It is no longer just a convenience to access data from anywhere. Using responsive design and flexible data structures allows access on all devices.

KISS - Keep it simple, silly. That philosophy applies to both design and coding. Well-commented, clean code is easier to write, debug, and maintain. It's all about finding the right balance.

My goal in life is to make the world paperless. It's one reason that I like programming! Being green or eco-friendly is just being less wasteful, which saves both time and precious resources.

I think organization is very important, both in the physical world and the digital world. What good is knowledge or data if it can't be accessed properly? Keeping it organized makes it useful.

"Two heads are better than one". When people work together, they make more connections than a single person could alone. I think that good teamwork is key to success.

Why do things manually or one at a time? Let technology do it more efficiently, like doing several tasks simultaneously, or running automatically behind the scenes.

Life should be fun. When I run into problems, I try to spice it up, like turning them into games. It's easier to do something if you make it enjoyable.
